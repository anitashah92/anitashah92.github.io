The problem

Excel and email — for enterprise-scale decisions.

GroupM's commerce team ran their entire RFI process through spreadsheets and email threads. Coca-Cola and 50+ retail partners were exchanging documents manually, with no shared workspace, no version control, and no visibility into where any evaluation stood.

The result: cycles that should take days stretched to weeks. Information got lost in inboxes. Leadership couldn't see pipeline status without chasing someone down.

"Designing for two very different users — the brands sending RFIs and the retailers receiving them — meant every screen had to serve two mental models at once."

Discovery

Two users. Two very different mental models.

Brand-side users (Coca-Cola's commerce team) needed to create RFIs, set deadlines, track response status across 50+ retailers, and export results for leadership reporting.

Retailer-side users needed a simple, fast way to receive RFIs, understand what was being asked, complete responses, and submit — without needing training or hand-holding.

Stakeholder interviews revealed the biggest pain wasn't the data itself — it was the handoff moments: sending, receiving, following up, and confirming completion. That's where Flow had to be airtight.

Design

One platform, two clear experiences.

RFI Builder — a step-by-step creation flow for brand teams. Section-by-section structure, reusable question templates, deadline setting, and retailer assignment in one guided flow.

Retailer response view — stripped-back and task-focused. Retailers see exactly what's being asked, can respond inline, save drafts, and submit with a clear confirmation state.

Status dashboard — live view of every active RFI, response rates per retailer, and overdue flags. Gave leadership visibility without needing to ask anyone.

Iteration

Tags to progress bars — a small change with big impact.

V1 used status tags on each RFI row. User testing showed brand-side users wanted a faster sense of overall completion — not just per retailer.

V1 — Status tags

Per-retailer status

Each row showed a tag: Sent, In Progress, or Complete. Required scanning the whole list to understand overall progress.

V2 — Progress bar

Cycle-level completion

A single progress bar per RFI showing % of retailers completed. Instant at-a-glance status without scanning individual rows.

Reflections

What I took from this project.

Design for the handoff

The hardest UX problems weren't in the core flows — they were in the moments between. Getting sends, confirmations, and status updates right was where trust was built.

Two users, one system

Keeping brand and retailer experiences distinct while sharing the same data model required constant alignment with engineering on how state was shared.

What I'd add

Email notification design — the comms layer outside the app was never properly designed. A lot of follow-up still happened in inboxes.

Build vs. buy

We evaluated third-party RFI tools before building. The decision to build was right — no off-the-shelf tool could handle the GroupM-specific workflow and partner scale.
