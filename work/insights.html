The problem

Gigabytes of retail data with no way to compare it.

GroupM's commerce team was manually assembling PowerPoint charts for Coca-Cola, Nestlé, and Unilever — pulling data from multiple sources across nine retail media channels for every client presentation. There was no single place it lived.

Strategists spent hours on report assembly instead of analysis. Asking "how does Walmart compare to Kroger across all channels?" meant building a new deck from scratch. Insights was built to fix that.

"The strongest design decisions aren't the ones that go unchallenged — they're the ones you can defend with evidence after being challenged."

Discovery

Understand the data before touching a frame.

Stakeholder sessions surfaced two non-negotiables: retailers needed fair comparison groups, and charts had to show both overall and channel-level performance.

Data audit mapped every metric flowing into Insights — overall scores, nine channel weights, monthly traffic, revenue, and growth rate. Chart types had to follow the data questions, not aesthetics.

Library alignment with engineering on Nivo Rocks early meant no designing charts that couldn't ship.

The dashboard

Four charts. Four questions. One page.

Treemap — Which retailers perform best overall? Sized by score, colored green. Engineering almost vetoed it — went back with research and it shipped.

Bubble heatmap — How do retailers compare across nine channels? (Replaced the radar chart after testing — see below.)

Bar chart — How does traffic and revenue trend month over month?

Scatter plot — Where should a brand invest next? Maps performance vs. growth rate. Became the go-to slide in Unilever pitches.

User testing

The pivot that made it actually useful.

Maze testing revealed the core problem: three retailers wasn't enough. Strategists needed five or more simultaneously — where radar chart polygons overlap into unreadable noise.

V1 — Radar chart

Works for 3 retailers

Nine axes, each retailer as a polygon. Elegant up to 3 — but strategists needed 5+, where it became unreadable.

V2 — Bubble heatmap

Handles 5+ with ease

Retailers as rows, channels as columns. Bubble size and color encode score. Hover highlights the full row and column for focus.

Reflections

What I took from this project.

Data viz is a research discipline

Every chart started with a question — what is the user trying to understand? Working backward from that, not aesthetics, prevented a lot of wrong turns.

Defend decisions with evidence

The treemap pushback was real pressure. Going back with research rather than capitulating shaped every design conversation since.

What I'd add with more time

AI-assisted callouts above each chart — "Walmart leads 6 of 9 channels, but lags in CTV." LLM integration conversations were promising.

Presentation mode

A full-screen, no-chrome mode for client meetings would have made Insights an even stronger pitch asset — a small change, big perceived value.
